 Organic farming can improve soil quality and provide effective pest control with reduced inputs compared to conventional farming . Although organic and conventional farming are often viewed as dichotomous they may overlap in pest management and soil quality goals and outcomes . Here we assessed similarities and differences between conventional and organic apple orchards in Washington State by quantifying pesticide program risk to natural enemies soil quality leaf nitrogen and abundance of woolly apple aphid
