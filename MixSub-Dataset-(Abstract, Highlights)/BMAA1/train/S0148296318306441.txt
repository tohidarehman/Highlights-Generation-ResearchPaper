 This study seeks to establish entrepreneurial marketing as a key construct that positively influences organizational performance . The authors review the evolution of the domain and conceptualization of EM and synthesize the literature that is emerging from the marketing entrepreneurship interface on this fertile research stream . They define EM and identify the conditions under which it yields better organizational performance outcomes . The moderating effect of network structure environmental variables and firm size is identified through several propositions that stem from the proposed conceptual framework .
