 Integrating social role theory and stakeholder theory with group diversity literature and using data from a sample of S P 1500 firms from 2007 to 2015 this study examines the boundary conditions under which female board representation increases firm performance through their positive influence on corporate social responsibility . Specifically the effects of female board representation on firm performance through CSR is most pronounced when female directors have a greater power to promote CSR and when the firm has the motivation to engage in CSR . Moreover this moderated mediation model holds especially for three dimensions of CSR shedding light on the nuances of what women bring to the table .
