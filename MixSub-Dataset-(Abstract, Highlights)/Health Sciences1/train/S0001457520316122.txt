 Attributions of the causes of accidents to human error are problematically reductive yet such attributions persist in media coverage . Few experiments have examined how human error attributions affect peoples perceptions . An experiment compared attributions of accidents to human error versus other causes . Participants

@highlight We manipulated attributions of accidents human error versus other causes in 50 real news stories.
@highlight Human error attributions resulted in greater endorsement of individual punishment.
@highlight Human error attributions resulted in less perceived organizational responsibility.
@highlight Human error attributions were perceived as more preventable retrospectively.
@highlight The pattern of results replicated in a second experiment.
