 There are large individual differences in dealing with everyday social stress . Therefore we investigated the association of social inhibition with the emotional and physiological responses to the Trier Social Stress Test . Undergraduate students social inhibition was associated with increased negative mood reactivity and heightened sympathetic activation . Physiological stress reactivity seems to be mostly adrenergic in women and also adrenergic in men . Emotional and physiological stress responses are associated with individual differences in social inhibition . This warrants more research on mechanisms that underlie the relations between social inhibition stress and health .
