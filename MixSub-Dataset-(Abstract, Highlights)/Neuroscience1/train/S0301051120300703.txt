 Past psychological theory and research suggest two opposing relationships between effort and reward valuation . Cognitive dissonance theory and research suggest that increased effort is associated with increased reward valuation whereas theory and research on effort discounting suggest that increased effort is associated with decreased reward valuation . The present study was designed to test these two opposing perspectives by examining a potential moderator of the relationship between effort and reward valuation the belief that the effort was necessary to obtain the reward .
