 The present research investigates the general effect of auditory noise control in individuals eating and drinking experiences . In particular the study applied passive vs active commercial headphone noise control techniques to an urban drinking situation . Here each participant drank twice the same coffee while exposed to a louder vs less loud version of the same background noise of a food court in busy hours . Note that by loud louder and less loud we are referring to differences in the sound level of the noise .
