 We study the price and welfare effects of collusion between two sided platforms and show that they depend on whether collusion occurs on both sides or a single side of the market and whether users single home or multi home . Our most striking result is that one sided collusion leads to lower prices on the collusive side if the cross group externalities exerted on the collusive side are positive and sufficiently strong .
