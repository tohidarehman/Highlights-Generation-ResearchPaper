 Increasingly adolescent young adult and adult children are relied upon as donors for their parents undergoing blood and marrow stem cell transplant . How family functioning impacts donors decision making and whether haploidentical donor children have unique supportive care needs is unknown . In this qualitative research study we conducted 15 semistructured telephone interviews among individuals who underwent blood or marrow stem cell donation for their parent .
