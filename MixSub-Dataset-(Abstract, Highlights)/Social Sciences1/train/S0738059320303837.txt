 This paper discusses the challenges teachers and ethnic minority students face living in rural North western Vietnam and how social entrepreneurship can mitigate these . The data was gathered through 33 interviews . The data were analysed using thematic analysis drawing from Frasers justice model . The paper also links to Granovetters social embeddedness concept to emphasise how social enterprise enabled socio economic networks can support ethnic minority students to overcome redistribution representation and recognition problems . The paper makes an original contribution by showing how socio economic networks enhance weak ties and bridge resources to empower the socially disadvantaged to achieve educational inclusion .
