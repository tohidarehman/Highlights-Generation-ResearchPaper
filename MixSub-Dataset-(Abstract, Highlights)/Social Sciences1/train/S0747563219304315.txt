 This paper explores how adolescents perceive the role and impact of Information and Communication Technology use in Cyber Dating Violence and whether CDV results in unique experiences or impacts compared to that of offline Adolescent Dating Violence . Fifty four adolescents aged 1316

@highlight Adolescents perceive ICT to have both enabling and disenabling aspects for victims and instigators of CDV.
@highlight ICT is viewed as enabling instigators of CDV by facilitating CDV behaviours.
@highlight ICT is viewed as enabling victims of CDV to distance themselves from report respond to and cope with indirect abuse.
@highlight ICT is viewed as disenabling victims of CDV due to its unique and potentially more harmful impact than offline abuse.
@highlight Implications are raised for educating adolescents about CDV and healthy responses to abusive experiences in relationships.
