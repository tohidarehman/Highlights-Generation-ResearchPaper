 Internet Gaming Disorder suggests a sustained problematic pattern of video gaming contributing to both physical and psychological harms . Applications of self determination theory have revealed two correlates of IGD daily frustration of basic needs and stronger extrinsic gaming motivations . Theory and recent research suggests that poorer self control may be one mechanism by which daily need frustration is associated with IGD and less adaptive gaming motivations .
