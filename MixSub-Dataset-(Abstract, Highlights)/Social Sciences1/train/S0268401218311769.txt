 Drawing from status quo bias theory and coping theory this study examines how the inertial use of incumbent web payment services influences users intention to use new mobile payment services . By conducting an online survey this study reveals that inertia demonstrates triple effects on intention to use MP services

@highlight This study highlights the triple effects of inertia in the web mobile payment transition context.
@highlight Transition costs sunk costs and loss aversion are important determinants of inertia.
@highlight Inertia directly decreases users intention to use mobile payment services.
@highlight Inertia leads to decreased perceived value and increased perceived threat.
@highlight Inertia positively moderates the effect of perceived controllability on intention to use mobile payment services.
