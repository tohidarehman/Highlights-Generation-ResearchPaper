 This article analyzes the European regulation REACH as a co management policy tool for chemical risks . Whilst the existing literature on the topic either highlights the strong capacity of authorities to force the industry to produce the data needed for decision making or criticizes the formers subordination to economic interests this article takes a new approach showing how regulators and the industry use REACH to engage in systematic compromise and a pedagogical approach to chemical risks .
