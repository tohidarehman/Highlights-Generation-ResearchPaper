 Continuous and comprehensive monitoring is one of the most important practices to trace changes in the state of the environment and target management efforts . Yet governmental resources are often insufficient for monitoring all required environmental parameters and therefore authorities have started to utilize citizen observations to supplement and increase the scale of monitoring . The aims of the present study were to show the potential of citizen science in environmental monitoring by utilising citizen observations of the non indigenous Harris mud crab
