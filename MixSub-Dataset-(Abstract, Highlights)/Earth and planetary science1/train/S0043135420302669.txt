 Recreational water quality guidelines protect the public from health risks associated with water recreation by helping to prevent unacceptable concentrations of pathogenic organisms in ambient water . However illness risk is associated with both the concentration of pathogens in the water and the degree of contact with those pathogens . Different recreational activities can result in different levels of contact with ambient water containing water borne pathogens .
