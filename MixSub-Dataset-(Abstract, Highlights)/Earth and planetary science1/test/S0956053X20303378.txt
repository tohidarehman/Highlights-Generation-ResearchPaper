 This study provides Sri Lanka wide empirical evidence on trends and determinants of waste disposal mechanisms at household level over the past decade from 2007 to 2016 . The study uses the data of 60 820 households from three waves of the Sri Lanka income and expenditure survey and estimates a multinomial logistic regression model . The descriptive statistics show that though Sri Lankan households predominantly burn waste there is an emerging trend of shifting the main mechanism more towards waste collection arrangements .
